ChessMate is a project centered around a smart chess board, incorporating various electronic components such as reed switches, 1N4148 diodes, RGB LEDs, resistors, NPN transistors, and an Arduino Mega microcontroller. These components work together to enhance the traditional chess experience by providing features like precise piece detection, illuminated moves, and intuitive gameplay. With ChessMate, players can enjoy a modern twist on the timeless game of chess, suitable for both beginners and seasoned enthusiasts alike.

Performed NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) mapping using Python, utilizing several libraries including matplotlib, numpy, rioxarray, geopandas, earthpy, and rasterio. The project involved analyzing satellite imagery to assess vegetation health and density. By calculating the NDVI, the analysis identified areas with dense vegetation (high NDVI values) and areas with sparse vegetation or bare soil (low NDVI values). The resulting maps provided valuable insights for environmental monitoring, land management, and agricultural practices.
